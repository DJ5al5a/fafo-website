Contact Us

Reach out for support, share your experience, or ask for guidance

We are here to listen. If you're going through a difficult time with the child welfare system, you can reach out to us for support and guidance.

Important: FAFO is Not a Law Firm

We do NOT provide legal advice or legal representation.

Contacting us does NOT create an attorney-client relationship. We are a community advocacy organization that provides educational information and support. For legal advice, consult a licensed attorney.

How We Can Help

  • Listen to your story and provide emotional support
  • Point you to relevant educational resources on this website
  • Help you understand where to find legal aid in your area
  • Explain how to file complaints with oversight agencies
  • Connect you with advocacy organizations when appropriate

Send Us a Message

Direct Email Contact

For privacy and security, you can email us directly at:

📧 familyandfriendoutreach@proton.me

Privacy Note: Email is the most private way to contact us. We use ProtonMail for encrypted email communication. Your message goes directly to us without third-party tracking or data collection.

What to Include in Your Email

  • Subject line: Brief description of your situation
  • Your county: Which Florida county are you in?
  • Your question or concern: What do you need help with?
  • How we can help: Are you looking for resources, support, or referrals?

Important Reminders

  • Do NOT include sensitive case details in email
  • Do NOT include children's full names
  • Do NOT include case numbers or court dates
  • This is NOT a confidential attorney-client communication

What to Expect After You Contact Us

  • Response Time: We will respond to your message as soon as possible, typically within 2-3 business days.
  • Confidentiality: We treat your information with respect. However, this is NOT a privileged attorney-client communication.
  • Support, Not Legal Advice: We can provide emotional support, educational resources, and referrals, but we cannot give you legal advice or represent you in court.
  • Crisis Situations: If you or your children are in immediate danger, call 911. Do not wait for our response.

If You Need Immediate Legal Help

If you're facing an emergency court hearing or your children have just been removed, contact a dependency attorney immediately:

Florida Bar Lawyer Referral: 1-800-342-8011

See our Resources page for legal aid organizations in your area.

You Don't Have to Face This Alone

We know what it feels like to be overwhelmed, confused, and isolated when dealing with the child welfare system.

This website was created by people who have been through this experience - people who had nowhere to turn and no one who understood. We built FAFO so that families like yours don't have to feel that way.

Whether you need someone to listen, help understanding your rights, guidance on finding legal representation, or support during one of the hardest times of your life - reach out. We're here.

You are not alone. We see you. We believe you. We're here to help.
