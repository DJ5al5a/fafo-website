Court Document Templates

Printable templates to help you organize your case and communicate with the court

⚖️ LEGAL DISCLAIMER - READ FIRST

FAFO is NOT a law firm and does not provide legal advice. These templates are for educational and organizational purposes only.

IMPORTANT:

  • Do NOT file any motion with the court without reviewing it with your attorney first
  • Court rules vary by county - your attorney knows the local requirements
  • These templates are general guides - your attorney will customize them for your specific case
  • Always consult with a qualified dependency attorney before submitting anything to the court

How to Use These Templates

  1. Click the template link to open it in a new window
  2. Right-click and "Print" or save as PDF
  3. Fill out by hand with a pen, OR type directly if you save as PDF first
  4. Review with your attorney before using or filing
  5. Keep copies of everything for your records

Pro tip: Print multiple copies of the tracking logs and keep them in your court binder. Fill them out weekly so you don't forget details.

📋 Tracking & Documentation Templates

These help you document everything that happens in your case

📅 Visit Log Template

Track every visit with your children - dates, times, activities, how they were doing, any concerns.

Why you need this: Shows the court you're consistent with visits and engaged with your children.

View Template ↓

📞 Communication Log Template

Document every interaction with DCF, case managers, service providers - phone calls, visits, emails.

Why you need this: Proves what DCF said, promised, or demanded. Critical for showing their failures.

View Template ↓

✅ Case Plan Progress Tracker

Track completion of every case plan task - when started, when completed, certificates received.

Why you need this: Shows the judge you're complying with the case plan and making progress.

View Template ↓

📝 Court Hearing Notes Template

Take notes during every court hearing - what was ordered, what was said, next hearing date.

Why you need this: Your memory isn't perfect. Write down what the judge ordered immediately.

View Template ↓

⚖️ Sample Motions & Letters

Templates to communicate with the court - MUST be reviewed by your attorney first!

⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING

DO NOT file these motions yourself without an attorney! Court procedures are technical and mistakes can hurt your case. These templates show you what's possible - your attorney will handle the actual filing.

📄 Sample Motion for Return of Children

Request that the court return your children to your care based on changed circumstances.

When to use: After you've completed services, addressed concerns, and believe home is safe.

View Template ↓

📄 Sample Motion to Modify Case Plan

Request changes to unreasonable or impossible case plan tasks.

When to use: When case plan tasks are duplicative, not available in your area, or unrelated to the case.

View Template ↓

✉️ Character Reference Letter Guide

Template and instructions for people writing reference letters for you.

Who should write: Employers, teachers, clergy, counselors, neighbors who know you well.

View Template ↓

📊 Progress Report to Court

Summary of your compliance with case plan to submit at hearings.

When to use: Before Judicial Review or Permanency hearings to show your progress.

View Template ↓
