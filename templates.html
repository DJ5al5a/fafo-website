Documentation Templates

Printable tools to help you organize and document your case

⚠️ READ THIS FIRST

We're not lawyers. These are forms to help you keep track of what's happening. That's it.

Don't Submit These to Court

  • These are for YOUR records - keep them in a binder or folder
  • Don't file these with the court - your lawyer handles that
  • Don't give these to DCF unless your attorney says to
  • Use them to remember dates, times, and what happened
  • Show them to your lawyer when you meet

Why use these? So you don't forget what happened. Court moves fast. DCF says one thing and does another. Write it down while it's fresh.

Forms to Track What's Happening

Print these out. Fill them in. Don't lose them.

📅 Visit Log

Track every visit with your kids - when, where, what you did, how they seemed.

Why: Shows the court you're staying involved.

📞 Communication Log

Write down every call, text, and meeting with DCF and case workers.

How to Use Rights Cards

  1. Print on cardstock (heavier paper) if possible
  2. Cut along the lines carefully
  3. Laminate if you have access to a laminator
  4. Keep in your wallet, on your fridge, or anywhere accessible
  5. Show to DCF workers if needed: "I want my attorney present"

Wallet-Sized Rights Card

Print on cardstock, cut along dotted lines, laminate if possible.

KNOW YOUR RIGHTS

When DCF Contacts You:

  • You have the RIGHT to remain silent
  • You have the RIGHT to an attorney
  • You have the RIGHT to refuse entry without a court order

What to Say:

"I want to cooperate, but I need my attorney present before I answer questions."

DO NOT:

  • Admit or consent to anything
  • Sign anything without reading
  • Let them pressure you

Document EVERYTHING

How to Use These Templates

  1. Click "Preview" to see what the template looks like
  2. Click "Print Template" or use your browser's print function (Ctrl+P / Cmd+P)
  3. Print multiple copies - you'll need more than one
  4. Fill out by hand with a pen as things happen
  5. Keep them organized in a binder or folder
  6. Share with your attorney when discussing your case

Remember:

  • These are for YOUR personal organization
  • Your attorney will prepare actual court filings
  • Keep templates updated weekly while case is active
  • Store in a safe place - these contain case details
